## FAQ Content

**Q: What is an Integrated Regional Water Management Plan?**
An IRWM Plan is a voluntary and comprehensive non-regulatory planning document prepared on a region-wide scale. It identifies broadly supported priority water resource projects and programs with multiple benefits, addressing supply, flood management, quality, environmental restoration, recreation, and land use.

**Q: How can my organization join the RWMG?**
Download the New Member Packet or DAC Membership Application from our website. The RWMG maintains Memoranda of Understanding establishing membership frameworks, with the most recent MOU from 2024.

**Q: When are board meetings held?**
Board meetings are held on the fourth Monday of each month at 1:30 PM via Zoom and telephone conference. Meeting agendas and minutes are posted on our website.

**Q: How is the RWMG funded?**
The RWMG secures state grants through programs like Proposition 50, 84, and 1. In 2011/2012, the group was awarded a $9.4 million implementation grant.

**Q: What area does the RWMG cover?**
The RWMG covers the entire Madera Region, including both foothill and valley areas of Madera County. This creates partnerships across water districts, cities, tribal communities, and disadvantaged communities.
